This is the very basic example. We see 10 items in the visible part of the viewport because of CSS: 10 items of 25px each are equal to 250px of the viewport height. Datasource.get log shows us that there were 3 requests of 5, 10 and 5 items on the initial load (before any scrolling). So we have 20 DOM elements initially: 15 downward items with positive indexes and 5 upward negative items. Our Datasource is unlimited, it produces items with indexes from -Infinity to +Infinity. The number of DOM elements in the viewport is not constant and fluctuates around the initial value during scrolling (20-31).

Why do we have 3 requests initially, why the first one is about only 5 items, why at least 10 items are invisible? This is how the *uiScroll works and further reading should shed light on the details. And we need to know that the default behaviour could be changed via Settings object.
